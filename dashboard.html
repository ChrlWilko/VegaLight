Melbourne Housing Data (2016-2017)

An in-depth look at the behaviour of the Melbourne housing market and the fluctuations across suburbs in pricing, house size and real estate agencies.

Prices Across the Suburbs

The map to right displays the median property prices for each suburb, highlighting the changes of value across the Greater Melbourne area. As made evident in the graphic, properties to the east of the CBD (red circle) generally have a higher median price. The suburbs surrounding the bay also have recorded higher median prices compared to northern and western suburbs. This comes as no surprise, due to Australians obsession with the beach and over 85% of our population living less than 1 hour from a beach (Wheeler, 2010); every street closer to the beach is a step in right direction.

The most expensive suburb was Kooyong, just to the east of the CBD with a median property price of $3,080,000.
The most affordable suburb was Kurunjang in the west, hosting a median property price of $362,000.

Seasonal Buying Patterns

There are many factors that go into when a person will decide to buy property, and the time of year is a crucial aspect that is rarely overlooked. The best time of year to buy is not set in stone, with many people suggesting that spring or autumn time provide the best opportunities (Inovayt, 2022). However, in Melbourne, this assumption does not necessarily hold true, with the greatest number of properties being sold between May through July.

*January data not available.

Most Prominent Real Estate Agencies

In Melbourne there are over 400 different real estate agencies (Domain, 2016) all competing for the sale of homes. Some of these agencies are more profound and take up a larger share of the housing market. These agencies also have holds on certain regions and suburbs of Melbourne. As seen in the graphic to the right, the agency Nelson maintain the greatest total number of properties sold across Melbourne, however, in the suburb of Albert Park they rank as the lowest seller.

Property Types Effect on the Number of Bedrooms, Price and Proximity to CBD over the Decades

The three main property categories in Australia are houses, townhouses and units. Houses are generally identified as seperated dwellings that come in all shapes and sizes. Houses are very popular in Australia, affording residents with privacy and flexibilty. A townhouses is a multi-level building that allows people to own a house on a strata title; which means they own the building but share the land with others. Units or apartments are self-contained living areas that are part of a larger building consisting of other units. (Realestate.com, 2022)
Most properties in Melbourne have between 2-4 bedrooms as the standard, with units generally having fewer and houses containing more. Prior to the 1960's most properties built were houses, but during the 60's and 70's, a boom in units being built can be seen. Townhouses only really began being built in larger numbers in the late 90's and early 2000's.
In the scatterplot below, the distance of a property is measured in kilometres from the Melbourne CBD. Unsurprisingly, units take up a large amount of the area in the bottom left of the graphic, showing their abundance in proximity to the city and greater affordability. Townhouses generally are seen a bit further from the city with a slight jump in price as well.
Houses demonstrate the greatest range and number, with a strong correlation between distance and price. The median price of living in a house 3.0 km from the CBD in Middle Park, is $5.6 million. Comparatively, the furthest distance from the CBD, 45.9 km in the suburb Gisborne, has a median house price of $775,000.
